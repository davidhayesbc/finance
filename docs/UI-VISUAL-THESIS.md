# Privestio UI Visual Thesis

## Objective

Define the visual doctrine for a complete UI refresh of Privestio before screen design starts.

This product is not a lifestyle budgeting app and not a loud trading terminal. It is a private financial operating surface for a household that wants clarity, control, and trust.

## Visual Thesis

Privestio should feel like a private wealth ledger rendered with editorial restraint: warm paper light, graphite structure, smoked-glass depth, and a single mineral accent that makes money movement feel precise rather than playful.

## Core Feeling

- Private, not social
- Domestic, not corporate
- Exact, not sterile
- Calm, not sleepy
- Premium, not luxurious-for-show

The interface should read like a serious personal instrument. It needs enough warmth to feel lived-in and enough rigor to make balances, forecasts, liabilities, and imports feel trustworthy.

## Art Direction

### Material World

The visual language should combine three materials:

- Paper light: soft bone and oat tones that make the product feel personal instead of datacenter-cold
- Graphite framing: sharp typography, thin separators, and dark data surfaces that give the interface authority
- Smoked glass: selective overlays, drawers, dialogs, and inspector panels with translucent depth rather than heavy card stacks

This should not become skeuomorphic. The materials are cues for mood and hierarchy, not literal textures pasted into the UI.

### Composition Bias

- Prefer wide layout planes over dashboard card mosaics
- Use large sectional bands, split workspaces, and dense list or table surfaces
- Let one number, one chart, or one workflow own each region
- Keep chrome thin and subordinate to the working surface
- Treat the first loaded screen as an instrument panel, not a marketing page

### Personality

The tone should suggest "confident household CFO" rather than "consumer finance app".

That means:

- more ledger and atlas
- less candy and gamification
- more editorial cropping and spacing
- less boxed widgets and synthetic polish

## Visual System Rules

### Typography

Use a two-family system:

- Primary sans: Familjen Grotesk for navigation, tables, labels, filters, and operational copy
- Secondary serif: Newsreader for high-importance figures, section intros, and occasional emphasis moments

The serif should be used sparingly. It exists to give the product distinction and gravity, not to turn routine product UI into an editorial spread.

### Color

Base palette:

- Bone `#F4EFE7`
- Oat `#E7DED0`
- Graphite `#1E2328`
- Coal `#111417`

Primary accent:

- Verdigris `#1E6B68`

Semantic accents:

- Gain `#2E7D57`
- Risk `#B24A3A`
- Forecast `#A07A2F`

Rules:

- Verdigris is the only brand accent
- semantic colors should appear as data signals, not decorative fills
- avoid bright fintech blues, purple gradients, and candy green success treatments

### Shape and Surface

- Sharp radii or near-square corners
- Thin dividers instead of boxed cards
- Large surfaces can layer with subtle transparency, but routine modules should stay mostly flat
- Shadows should be rare and soft enough that removing them would not collapse the design

## Image Thesis

There are no suitable uploaded images in the repository for this refresh beyond app icons, so the UI direction should assume custom-generated or custom-sourced imagery later.

When imagery is introduced, it should follow this brief:

- macro still-life photography language
- daylight across desk materials, paper, glass, metal, and shadow
- no embedded UI frames, dashboards, charts, or app screenshots inside the image
- no visible logos, signage, or typographic clutter
- enough calm tonal area to place strong text over part of the composition

The image role is atmospheric authority, not explanation. The product surface itself should carry the explanation.

## What This Refresh Must Avoid

- pastel fintech softness
- dark-mode trading terminal aesthetics
- homepage-style hero copy inside product screens
- card grids as the default layout strategy
- oversized KPI tiles competing with actual workflows
- ornamental gradients behind routine tables, forms, and filters
- icon-heavy decoration that does not improve scanning

## Screen-Level Implications

If this thesis is applied correctly, the product should evolve toward:

- a left navigation rail that feels architectural, not app-store generic
- dense but calm dashboards built from bands, lists, and charts instead of tile farms
- account detail pages that feel like dossiers with strong tabular hierarchy
- import and rules screens that read like operational workbenches
- forecasting and budgeting screens that feel analytical and deliberate, not gamified

## Litmus Test

The refreshed UI is successful if a user can open Privestio and immediately feel three things:

1. their data is private
2. the system is serious
3. the product is designed for long-term financial stewardship, not short-term engagement tricks

## Next Design Step

Translate this thesis into two follow-on artifacts:

1. a content plan for the key screens
2. an interaction thesis covering motion, transitions, and state feedback
